Here's what you can do about it...

Below are links to very easy-to-use privacy tools and opt-out pages of major digital advertising groups and social networks.

Companies won't respect your personal beliefs unless you make them known. Make your browser work for you by taking advantage of these tools and opt-outs!

Privacy Tools

Tracker Blockers

Ghostery, uBlock Origin (for Chrome and Firefox), Lightbeam for Firefox, and Privacy Badger are very useful tools that prevent unwanted tracking from advertising companies and keep your online activity safe. I recommend Privacy Badger along with uBlock Origin for a first time user, as uBlock Origin is very lightweight and Privacy Badger has a very intuitive user experience that teaches you about trackers as you use it.

Secure Communications

The Electronic Frontier Foundation, a non-profit organization that defends web privacy, along with The Tor Project, has built HTTPS Everywhere, a tool that rewrites requests sent from your browser to use HTTPS always, rather than HTTP.

Do Not Track

Although there is not a universal solution to web privacy yet, many companies will respect a "Do Not Track" browser header. Learn how to set it up here!

Advertiser Opt-Outs

Visit the Digital Advertising Alliance's Consumer Choice Page and Ghostery Enterprise and follow the instructions to opt-out of tracking for interest-based ads by all companies participating in each page. Note that these opt-outs will only work if you have not already disabled third-party cookies.

Social Network Preferences

Facebook and Twitter have internal opt-outs that keep those networks from tracking your data as well. Follow the links for whichever network(s) you are a part of!
